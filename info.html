Mike Tully lives and works in New York. He works independently and in collaboration with artists, architects, institutions, and publishers on works spanning artifacts, exhibitions, identities, publications, websites, and writing.

In 2022, he graduated with an MFA from the Yale School of Art, where he was awarded the Critical Practice Research Fellowship and the Beinecke Rare Book and Manuscript Library Research Fellowship.

He has worked as the Design Director for the Brooklyn Rail and with The New York Times, Wolff Olins, Lincoln Center, and various New York-based studios. In addition to his studio practice, he occasionally teaches, lectures, facilitates workshops, and visits as a critic at various art programs across the US. In spring of 2023, he was artist-in-residence at the Internet Archive in San Francisco.
