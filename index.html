Mike Tully lives and works in New York. He works independently and in collaboration with artists, architects, institutions, and publishers on projects spanning artifacts, exhibitions, publications, radio, research, sound, writing, and websites.

In 2022, he graduated with an MFA from the Yale School of Art, where he was awarded the Critical Practice Research Fellowship and the Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library Research Fellowship.

⎅

Recent projects include a sound walk, A Green Loop, in New Haven, an exhibition, No White Walls, at the Yale School of Art with Salvador Andrade Arévalo, Zoila Andrea Coc-Chang, Amartya De, Miguel Gaydosh, and athena quispe, a digital book, A Bewildering Minute, for the Yale School of Art Photography Department, an annual publication, Retrospecta 44, with Immanuel Yang and Alvin Ashiatey for the Yale School of Architecture, ongoing research on the Community Memory Project, a screensaver with tarah douglas for Aria Dean’s lecture at the Yale School of Art, a typeface revival and reinterpretation of French Roman, a poster on Workers’ Rights with Pete Gamlen and the Philadelphia Legal Association for CUP, and a short book by Nora N. Khan entitled Seeing, Naming, Knowing for the Brooklyn Rail.
